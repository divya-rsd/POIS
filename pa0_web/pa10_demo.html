Length-Extension vs HMAC
A naive MAC constructed as H(k || m) is vulnerable to length-extension attacks if H is a Merkle-Damgård hash. HMAC fixes this using a nested structure: H(k ⊕ opad || H(k ⊕ ipad || m)).
